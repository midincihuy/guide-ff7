III. Summary
--------------------

Let me explain what this guide is and is not:

  - This guide is NOT a rumor mill about the 7777s trick. 'Lucky Sevens' is a 
    well documented feature of the original game, and all other incarnations 
    (PC, PSP/PS3(PSN), NTSC/PAL, etc).

  - This guide is NOT a collection of various methods detailing ways to get 
    the 'Lucky Sevens' status. There are several other guides that have shown 
    other ways to do this, and they are all pretty accurate, if not cumbersome
    digging around for the info.

  - This guide IS in fact a detailed instruction and implementation tool.
    The method I use is perfectly legitimate, requires no GameShark or 
    emulation environment. It does not depend on luck or timing or any 
    excessively weird gameplay setups. It is simple, and quick.

  - This guide includes an extensive list for every potential xx77s combo. I 
    prepared it using a script in Excel, and then transferred it to text 
    format. (See Section XI for the complete list, after reading Section VI)

  - This guide has been tested most recently on my PSP(PSN) version of FF VII, 
    and I had used this same method on my PS1 ages ago, so it should work. I 
    have not tested it on PC, though there should not be any reason it 
    wouldn't work (if someone can verify that platform please tell me!).

IV. Getting Started
--------------------

So you are interested in easily performing the 'Lucky Sevens' trick? You have 
come to the right place!

This trick can be performed by any character (or several at once), in nearly 
any circumstance, provided you satisfy the following conditions:

   1. Character has at least 3889 Nominal HP (roughly lvl 50+)

        (See Definitions in the next Section  for a complete 
    list of important terms.)
         
   2. Sufficient HP Plus materia, if necessary

        (Usually two Mastered (+50%) is enough, or several mid-level HP Plus)

   3. Sufficient stock of Red/Green Materia

        (Red and Green materia impose an HP penalty of -2%, -5%,  
    or -10% ... it is necessary to have enough of these to 
    equip and  'force' a 77s combination. Generally, 6-7 of 
    each of -2%, -5%, and -10% is sufficient)

   4. Sufficient Materia slots in Weapon and Armor

    (The more the better. Some scenarios will require only a couple 
    Materia slots, while others may require 10 slots or more).

   5. Lots of Potions and Hi-Potions
    
    (this should be easy... W-Item if you are cheap, Item 
    shop if you are lazy)

   6. Menu Screen Accessible
    
    (If it's not obvious, you must be able to change your Materia 
    and/or your equipment via the Menu; as such you cannot do this 
    during battle. See section VII, Tips, for an exception in battle.)

   7. It is also advised you have this guide handy :D


One last note:

    Although certain weapons/armors and Materia may need to be 
    equipped to enable 'Lucky Sevens', all these can be swapped 
    out for your preferred gear after completing the 7777s setup, 
    and prior to battle. The only exception would be any HP Plus 
    Materia needed to keep your Max HP > 7777.

V. Definitions
--------------------

This is a list of terms that may or may not be familiar to you, but 
are helpful throughout this guide. 

   1. Nominal HP      - the base Max HP of any character without any Materia
              equipped

   2. Current HP      - current HP, as equipped      
                            (Current HP / Max HP)
                            (      xxxx / yyyy  )
   3. Max HP          - maximum HP, as equipped      


   4. 77s Combo       - one or more possibilities where last two digits are
                        '77' as in: 2377, 4777, 6077, 9277, xx77, etc.

   5. %change         - the sum of *all* HP bonuses and penalties from Materia
                (up to +50% from HP Plus, Green/Red -2%, -5%, -10%)

VI. Description
--------------------

If you have satisfied all the requirements in the 'Getting Started' section, 
then you should be ready to put this into practice. Remember, you can do this
trick any time you have access to the Menu Screen outside of battle. It's 
fairly simple and only requires a minute or two scanning the included List 
in Section XI.


Anyway, now for the Step by Step Instructions:

   1.   Using the List in Section XI (at the end of this guide), scan for 
    the corresponding HP value that matches the Nominal HP of the 
    character you wish to impart 'Lucky Sevens' on. (to find Nominal HP, 
    remove all Materia from the character)

   2.   If the 77s column (in parenthesis) is greater than 0, proceed to 
    step 3. If it's (0), you will be unable to use this method with 
    that character (until his/her Nominal HP changes, by leveling up;
    alternatively, see Section IX for another similar method using MP).

   3.   Choose a %change value listed to the right of your Nominal HP value. 

    ***** See Tips in the next Section for an example. *****

   4.   Equip Materia in combinations of +10%, -10%, -5%, and -2% equivalent 
    to the %change necessary. This should force a 77s combo that is 
    less than 7777. See Tips for more examples.

   5.   Use curative (Cure1, Cure2, Cure3, FullCure) magic or potions to fill 
    the character's Current HP = 77s combo (if not already full).

   6.   Remove the Green/Red Materia to increase your Max HP, use an HP Plus 
    Materia (or several) if necessary in order to get Max HP > 7777.

   7.   Use Potions (+100 hp) and/or Hi-Potions (+500 hp) to incrementally 
    increase your Current HP to *exactly* 7777.

   8.   Equip any new equpipment or Materia as desired (be certain your 
    Max HP does not subsequently fall below 7777 or you will have to 
    redo everything all over again.)

   9.   Engage in a battle, sit back, relax, and watch the pretty colors while 
    you unleash a flurry of attacks (64 of them) at 7777 damage each!


Just in case you forget, you should heal yourself after battle because you 
will be at 1 hp. (Or, alternatively, repeat steps 4-9 for more mayhem!)

VII. Tips
--------------------

1. Here is a sample of data from the table at the end, for Nominal HP values
from 4500 to 4509. I will demonstrate some examples below:


Nominal 77s 1   2   3   4   5   6
---------------------------------------------------------

4500    (0)                             
4501    (0)                             
4502    (5) +35%    +15%    -05%    -25%    -45%            
4503    (2) -45%    -65%                        
4504    (1) -65%                            
4505    (2) +46%    -65%                        
4506    (1) +26%                            
4507    (3) +06%    -14%    -85%                    
4508    (3) +57%    -14%    -85%                    
4509    (2) +37%    -85%    


     -> if my Nominal HP is 4500 or 4501, I cannot use this method to force 
    'Lucky Sevens'. bummer. **** update: see Section IX for possible
    solution using HP<->MP Materia.

     -> if my Nominal HP is 4504, I have just one choice to force 77s, 
    specifically I would have to equip a penalty of -65% HP, using 
    Red/Green Materia to force xx77. (In this case, 1577 HP.)

     -> if my Nominal HP is 4502, I have 5 choices, namely +35%, +15%, 
    -05%, -25%, and -45%. The easiest would be to equip just a Red 
    or Green Materia with a -5% HP penalty! The others will also 
    work, too.



2. In order to minimize the number of slots needed, use the greater penalty 
Red/Green Materia first (-10%), followed by the (-5%) and lastly (-2%). It 
doesn't matter if you use red or greens, just so long as you have the right 
combination so that the sum of all materia gives the appropriate %change. 

For Example:

                   Red/Green Materia               %change
            --------------------------------      ---------
             -10% - 10% - 5% - 2% - 2% - 2%    =    -31%

             -10% -  5% - 2% - 2% - 2% - 2%    =    -23%

     -10% - 10% - 10% - 10% - 10% - 5% - 2%    =    -57%


In the off-chance you find the %change to be -1% or -3%, or greater 
than 0%, you will need to equip at least one HP Plus Materia. 

For Example:


  HP Plus          Red/Green Materia               %change
  -------   --------------------------------      ---------
   (10%)  -  (5% + 2% + 2% + 2%)               =     -1%

   (10%)  -  (5% + 2% + 2% + 2% + 2%)          =     -3%

   (50%)  -  (10% + 5% + 2% + 2% + 2% + 2%)    =    +27%


3. If for some reason you do not wish to activate the 'Lucky Sevens' trick
immediately upon entering the next battle, instead you may complete all
steps as normal until Step 7; then simply use all but one Potion to leave 
the character's Current HP at 7677. When you wish to activate 'Lucky Sevens', 
just have any character feed them a single Potion, and voila! Of course, that 
character will be subject to any damage he/she may incur from any enemies, 
but that would have been the case with any other method, so this is still 
very helpful.


4. This is more of a caveat: There is not always necessarily a potential 
77s combo that will allow for 'Lucky Sevens' to be prepared with this method.
I've only encountered this maybe 5% of the time when checking my characters. 
If this does occur, the only solution is to level up, in turn giving you a 
new, higher Nominal HP (save and reset if it is again a bunk value). If you 
are already level 99, then you are out of luck (with that character). You'll 
have to get 'Lucky Sevens' the old fashioned way(s), or use another character.

*note, after running the Excel script, and a countif() function, it appears 
the actual proportion of incompatible HP values is as much as 25%. In 
practice it seems to be less than that, however.

**2nd note: See Section IX for an alternative way to accomplish a 77s combo
for a character whose Nominal HP has no %change available. You will need the
HP<->MP materia to use that method, but otherwise it's the same.

VIII. Calculations
--------------------

I began this project using Google Docs (spreadsheets) thinking I could 
share the table with everyone, who would then have access to fiddle around 
with it as they please. Unfortunately this wasn't the best way to present 
the data, so I backpedaled and converted it to Excel, wrote a VBA script, 
and then presented all the necessary information as in the List in 
Section XI, using simple text.

For those that may be interested, I will make an effort to describe the math 
involved in calculating the spreadsheet. In all honesty, it's actually quite 
simple, just a bit veiled as is any calculation in a typical RPG.

Starting with your Nominal (base) HP, the game will modify your Max HP 
depending on which Materia you equip. HP Plus materia can be +10%, +20%, 
+30%, +40%, or +50%, depending what AP level you have earned. Red and 
Green Materia all have a penalty of -2%, -5%, or -10% hp, generally the 
stronger/rarer materia have a greater penalty.

I will use 'x' as an intermediate variable (aka %change) in calculating 
Max HP from Nominal HP.

1.    x  = sum(HP Plus bonuses - Red/Green Materia penalties) = %change

    1a. the game limits -100% < %change < 100%, so apply 
        this limit if exceeded  

2. if x >= 0, then: Max HP = floor((Nominal HP)*(1+x))

3. if x <  0, then: Max HP = ceiling((Nominal HP)*(1+x))

    3a. Max HP is also subject to a limit of course:   10 < Max HP < 9999


example:

    Nominal HP = 6118, equipped with: 
    HP Plus bonuses HP+50%, HP+20%
    Green Materia -10%, -10%, -2%, -2%, -2%
    Red Materia   -10%, -10%, -5%, -2%, -2%

    x = sum((50 + 20) - (10 + 10 + 2 + 2 + 2) - (10 + 10 + 5 + 2 +2))
    x = sum((70%) - (26%) - (29%))
    x = +15% change

    because x > 0,

    Max HP = floor(6118*(1+0.15))
    Max HP = floor(7035.70)
    --> Max HP = 7035

    NEXT we will remove an HP Plus Materia (+50%)

    x = sum((20) - (10 + 10 + 2 + 2 + 2) - (10 + 10 + 5 + 2 +2))
    x = sum((20%) - (26%) - (29%))
    x = -35% change

    because x < 0

    Max HP = ceiling(6118*(1-0.35))
    Max HP = ceiling(3976.70)
    --> Max HP = 3977

    And thus, by merely changing one Materia, you can end up 
    with a 77s combo!!

    FYI, this scenario is a unique 77s combination for the Nominal HP 
    value of 6118 hp; I had it prescribed to work out this way, 
    for the purpose of demonstrating the HP calculations ;)



After setting up this formula into a table, I was able to check all 
%change values from +100% to -100%, for any particular Nominal HP value. 
Then I wrote an Excel script to evaluate and read every possible value 
into a simple, readable format. 

First I was using Google Docs (spreadsheets), so I could share with 
others. However, it was a bit too cumbersome, and didn't seem to be 
very quick in response. So, after trying a script in javascript, I had

IX. Additional Stuff
--------------------


As I wrote this, I considered the effect on other playthroughs, such as 
challenges. Considering 'Lucky Sevens' is almost always a guaranteed 
victory, there's not much challenge to using this technique. Also, since
most challenges out there are low-level, this won't even interfere with 
those rules, as this only applies once you get into the higher levels near
fifty or so.

A challenge might be to play a game to level 99, and carefully calibrate
every character's final Nominal HP to be one of those listed in the Bonus
Table at the end. This way, every character could be fixed at 7777 Max HP
always. Though that would be insanely powerful, it would certainly be a
challenge to attempt it (not even sure if it's possible in every case...)

Anyway, this guide is more just to play around with the game if you've 
already seen most of the rest of the content. As I said, this game has
an incredible amount of depth if you are creative in your playthrough!

UPDATE!

If you find a character unable to acquire a 77s combo using the normal 
method detailed above, then you may still be in luck!

By adjusting your Max MP (again, using Red/Green Materia), you can force
a 77s combo with MP and then switch that to your HP using HP<->MP Materia!

It's basically the same technique as above, except you are limited due to
the fact that you cannot decrease your Max MP using any Materia. So all 
possible values will be positive. At higher levels, the likelihood a
77s combo exists is diminished, because there are only so many available;
i.e. 77, 177, 277, 377, 477, 577, 677, 777, 877, 977.

I have posted a follow-up MP List after the Nominal HP list in Section XI.
Basically, you follow the same procedure outlined in the Description,
but for your MP. When you get a 77s combo on your MP, then you apply the 
HP<->MP Materia, and you now have a 77s combo on your HP! Cool, huh?
